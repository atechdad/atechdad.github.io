How I Fixed My Inflatable Christmas Tree Lawn Ornament

29 Nov 2014

So– today I took out the Christmas decorations. It was only during the installation ritual that I remembered that the fan in the inflatable tree we purchased last year had stopped working a week after we put it in. I also remembered that I, having thrown away the box and receipt– and being stubborn, had meant to fix it.
DISCLAIMER:

I am not an electrician or anything of the sort. I may not even be a person from which you should be getting advice. This is what I did to get this working. Don’t follow these instructions. If you do, I do not take any responsibility for the outcome.

Now that that’s said, here’s how I got this classy lawn ornament working again:

I checked the voltage at the adapter- everything checked out. I swapped the adapter anyway with a known good adapter. No luck.  I popped open the case and give it a once over. Aside from being unimpressed, I didn’t see anything that stood out to explain the failure.

I really didn’t feel like testing every component on the board. I began to wonder– the fan was rated at 12v, 1.5a. I then wondered if another 12v fan could be used in its place.

Finding a suitable fan took several tries. Many of the fans I had were under-powered and could not inflate the tree. The fan I settled on was sourced from an old power supply.

What I needed:

  • Power Supply Fan
  • Glue – A kind of Epoxy
  • Screwdriver
  • Hot Glue
  • Wire Nuts
  • Electrical Tape

Make sure everything is unplugged.

Use the screwdriver to open the fan’s enclosure. On the model I played with, there were also some clips

Clip the wires at the fan. Strip them

Make sure you’ve identified positive and negative leads. If you haven’t– take a second and do so.

Wire the new fan into the leads. Use the wire nuts. At this point, I used the hot glue to seal the nuts and electrical tape to hold it together. I know some folks are probably screaming about waterproofing right about now. I understand, it’ll be difficult for me to pass QC like original:

 

Now, if you are like me, you’ll find while the fan works, but you lose a lot of air. Something about a square peg and a round hole.  I found something to minimize the air loss here — an old koozie worked.

Glue it all up. I used the epoxy to hold everything together and the hot glue to seal everything. I used electrical tape to hold everything in place while the glue set.

Plug it up.

My final product:

Here is the tree, powered and inflated.

 

And that’s how I fixed my classy inflatable lawn ornament tree thing. My neighbors will be so happy!
